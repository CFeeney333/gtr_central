Nissan GTR R34

The Nissan GTR R34 is the successor to the R33 and the predecessor of the R35. Produced from 1999 to 2002, the R34 is widely regarded as the pinnacle of the Skyline GT-R lineup. With its aggressive styling, advanced technology, and impressive performance, the R34 remains a favorite among car enthusiasts and collectors.

Variants and Models

There were several different models of the R34, including:

  • GT-R V-Spec: The V-Spec (Victory Specification) model featured an upgraded suspension, Brembo brakes, an active limited-slip differential, and an advanced display unit for performance data monitoring.
  • GT-R V-Spec II: Introduced in 2000, the V-Spec II had further refinements, including a carbon fiber hood, improved aerodynamics, and interior upgrades.
  • GT-R N1: Designed for racing purposes, this model featured a strengthened engine, lightweight components, and a stripped-down interior. Only a limited number of N1 models were produced.
  • GT-R M-Spec: Aimed at a more luxurious experience, the M-Spec featured a softer suspension, leather seats, and heated front seats.
  • GT-R Nür: Named after the Nürburgring racetrack, this model featured an upgraded engine, larger turbochargers, and various performance enhancements. The Nür was available in both V-Spec II and M-Spec versions.
  • GT-R Z-Tune: Produced by Nissan's in-house tuning division, Nismo, this model featured a highly tuned engine producing 500 horsepower, enhanced suspension, and exclusive Nismo body parts. Only 20 Z-Tune models were built, making it one of the rarest and most sought-after R34 models.
